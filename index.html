Draining Accountability: Corruption and Flood Management in the Philippines

Cling Alinsonorin
Failed Field-Writer
Corruption has repeatedly undermined efforts to protect Philippine communities from recurrent flooding, turning vital infrastructure projects into channels for waste and impunity. From irregular procurement and substandard construction to political patronage, weaknesses in governance reduce the effectiveness of flood control measures and exacerbate disaster risk. This essay examines how corrupt practices shape flood management outcomes and proposes measures to strengthen transparency, accountability, and resilience.
